*   **Optimize energy consumption:** Automatically adjusting HVAC and lighting systems based on occupancy and environmental conditions.
*   **Improve indoor air quality:** Monitoring and controlling ventilation rates to ensure healthy indoor environments.
*   **Enhance comfort:** Providing personalized temperature and lighting settings for occupants.
*   **Reduce maintenance costs:** Detecting and diagnosing equipment problems early on.

The data collected by the BMS can be used to identify areas for improvement and optimize building performance over time. This technology can be readily adapted for use in smart homes, providing homeowners with greater control over their energy consumption and indoor environment.
